# Go-To-Market Strategy

## Exact Target User

**Primary:** Engineering Manager or CTO at a 5–30 person seed-to-Series A SaaS startup.
- Title: "Head of Engineering", "CTO", "VP Engineering", or "Engineering Manager"
- Company stage: Raised $500k–$5M, has 3–10 engineers
- Pain: Approves SaaS subscriptions without a clear picture of team-wide AI tool spend
- Budget authority: Signs off on $0–$2k/mo software without finance approval

**Secondary:** Founder-engineers who wear both the "technical" and "financial" hats at companies <10 people.

**Who it is NOT:** Enterprise IT buyers, solo indie hackers (too small to matter), companies >200 people (they have finance teams).

## What They Google/Scroll Before Wanting This Tool

- "cursor vs github copilot 2025" (decision research → tool overlap risk)
- "cursor pro vs business plan" (trying to pick the right plan → uncertainty about seat count)
- "ai tools for developers cost" (general spend awareness)
- Scrolling their bank statement and seeing "$600 AI subscriptions" as a line item
- Seeing a tweet like "does anyone actually use all 4 AI tools they're paying for"

## Where They Hang Out Online

- **r/ExperiencedDevs**, **r/startups**, **r/SideProject** — credible engineering advice communities
- **Indie Hackers** (indiehackers.com) — early-stage founders discussing tools and spend
- **Hacker News** — "Show HN" posts land well if the tool is genuinely useful and free
- **X/Twitter** — Following @swyx, @levelsio, @patio11, @benedictevans — tech/founder adjacent
- **Slack:** "Ramen Profitable" workspace, "Indie Founders" Slack, "Buildspace" alumni channels
- **Discord:** Buildspace, Supabase community, Vercel community (developers choosing SaaS)

## First 100 Users in 30 Days — $0 Budget

**Week 1: Warm launch**
1. Post on Indie Hackers "I Built" section with a specific dollar amount in the headline: "I built a free tool that found $480/mo in AI subscription waste for my startup — link"
2. DM 20 founders in my network directly with: "I built something that might save you money on AI tools — 2 min, no login" + their name + specific tool I know they use

**Week 2: HN Show HN**
3. Submit "Show HN: SpendLens — free AI tool spend auditor (no login)" on a Tuesday/Thursday 9am ET. Body: specific savings number found in beta + link. Respond to every comment within 30 minutes.
4. Post on r/ExperiencedDevs: "How we audited our AI tool spend and found $400/mo in waste" — narrative post, link in comments if allowed.

**Week 3: Leverage the tool's output**
5. Every user who submits their email gets a follow-up: "Would you share your audit result on X? The link strips your company details." Offer to retweet from @credex_rocks.
6. Find 5 engineering bloggers who write "tools I use" posts. Cold email with: "Your readers might save money with this — I'll write a guest post about AI tool spend patterns we've found."

**Week 4: Compound**
7. If HN landing in top 10: respond to all comments, post a "what we learned from 1,000 audits" thread on X with real aggregate data.

## The Unfair Distribution Channel

Credex already talks to companies that are *overspending* on AI credits (that's how they source discounted inventory). Every Credex customer conversation is a warm lead for SpendLens. The sales team can say: "Before we talk about credits, have you run a spend audit? Here: [link]. It takes 2 minutes and will show you exactly where Credex can help."

This is a channel no other audit tool has: a sales team that actively pitches the product as part of their discovery process.

## Week-1 Traction — What "Working" Looks Like

- 200+ unique audit completions (not just page views)
- 40+ email captures (20% conversion from completions)
- 1 HN comment from a real user with a specific savings number
- 3+ organic shares on X (screenshot of results, no prompting)
- 1 inbound request to the Credex consultation form from a SpendLens user
